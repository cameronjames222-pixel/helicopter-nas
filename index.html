CLASS A

IFR Exclusive
  • Dimensions 18,000' MSL up to & including FL 600
  • ATC Entry Requirement ATC Clearance Required
  • Minimum Pilot Certificate Instrument-Rated (Private/Commercial)
  • Required Equipment Mode C/S Transponder, Two-way Radio, ADS-B Out
VFR Weather Minimums VFR Not Applicable / IFR Only
Class A Deep Dive:
  • Altimeter Setting: Must set altimeter to 29.92 Hg upon entering.
  • Flight Rules: 1000% IFR only. No VFR flight or VFR-on-top allowed.
  • Speed Limit: Mach 1.0 maximum unless explicitly authorized.

CLASS B

"Bravo Big"
  • Dimensions SFC to tailored altitude (typically 10,000' MSL)
  • ATC Entry Requirement Explicit ATC Clearance Required
    ("Cleared to enter Class Bravo")
  • Minimum Pilot Certificate Private Pilot (or Student with specific endorsement, EXCEPT at restricted primary airports under App D)
  • Required Equipment Two-way Radio, Mode C/S Transponder with ADS-B Out
3 SM Visibility, Clear of Clouds
Class B Deep Dive:
  • The Magic Phrase: You cannot enter until ATC explicitly says your tail number and "Cleared to enter Class Bravo." Hearing "Radar contact" is NOT an entry clearance.
  • Airspeed Limits: 250 knots max inside the Bravo. Crucially for helicopters, 200 knots max in the airspace underlying the Class B shelf or in a VFR corridor.
  • Local Restriction: Student pilot takeoffs/landings are completely banned at primary Appendix D airports (e.g., KIAD, KJFK, KMCO, KLAX).

CLASS C

"Crowded Cities"
  • Dimensions SFC to 4,000' AGL (Core: 5 NM SFC-4k; Shelf: 10 NM 1,200'-4,000' AGL)
  • ATC Entry Requirement Establish Two-way Radio Comm
    (ATC responds using your tail number)
  • Minimum Pilot Certificate Student Pilot Certificate minimum
  • Required Equipment Two-way Radio, Mode C/S Transponder with ADS-B Out
3 SM Visibility, 3-152 (500' below, 1000' above, 2000' horizontal)
Class C Deep Dive:
  • Two-Way Contact Rule: Entry is legal the moment ATC reads back your tail number (e.g., "Helicopter 246 Papa, standby"). If they say "Aircraft calling standby" without your tail number, you must stay clear.
  • Standard Dimensions: Core extends from surface to 4,000' AGL with a 5 NM radius. Shelf extends from 1,200' to 4,000' AGL with a 10 NM radius.
  • Airspeed Limit: 200 knots maximum at or below 2,500' AGL within 4 NM of the primary airport.

CLASS D

"Dialogue"
  • Dimensions SFC to 2,500' AGL (Tailored lateral dimensions)
  • ATC Entry Requirement Establish Two-way Radio Comm
    (ATC must acknowledge you specifically)
  • Minimum Pilot Certificate Student Pilot Certificate minimum
  • Required Equipment Two-way Radio
3 SM Visibility, 3-152 (500' below, 1000' above, 2000' horizontal)
Class D Deep Dive:
  • Two-Way Contact Rule: Same as Class C. ATC must acknowledge your specific callsign to legally enter.
  • Standard Dimensions: Core extends from surface to 2,500' AGL with a tailored lateral radius (typically 4 to 5 NM).
  • Satellites & Cutouts: Look closely at Sectional charts for dashed box cutouts indicating where Class D changes shape or handoffs occur.
  • Airspeed Limit: 200 knots maximum at or below 2,500' AGL within 4 NM of the primary airport.

CLASS E

"Everywhere Else"
  • Dimensions Controlled airspace that isn't A, B, C, or D (Typically begins at 1,200' AGL or 700' AGL up to but not including 18,000' MSL)
  • ATC Entry Requirement No VFR communication requirement
  • Minimum Pilot Certificate Student Pilot Certificate minimum
  • Required Equipment No specific VFR requirements below 10,000' MSL. Mode C/S Transponder & ADS-B Out required ≥ 10,000' MSL and > 2,500' AGL
VFR Weather (< 10,000' MSL) 3 SM, 3-152 (500' below, 1000' above, 2000' horiz)
VFR Weather (≥ 10,000' MSL) 5 SM, 5-111 (1000' below, 1000' above, 1 SM horiz)
Class E Deep Dive:
  • The Floor Variants: Usually starts at 1,200' AGL (printed as magenta shading zipper lines) or 700' AGL (fuzzy magenta vignette edges surrounding airports with instrument approaches).
  • VFR Freedom: No radio communications or clearance required for VFR aircraft.
  • High Altitude Equipment Shift: The moment you hit 10,000' MSL, your cloud clearances spike to 5-111, and a Mode C transponder and ADS-B Out become mandatory.

CLASS G

"Go for It"
  • Dimensions Surface up to overlying controlled airspace (Class E)
  • ATC Entry Requirement Uncontrolled Airspace (No ATC contact required)
  • Required Equipment No specific VFR requirements
  • Special Helicopter Exception 14 CFR 91.155(b)(1): Helicopters may operate at less than 1/2 SM visibility if flying at a speed that allows adequate opportunity to see and avoid traffic or obstructions.
Helicopter Weather Minimums Grid
≤ 1,200' AGL (DAY) 1/2 SM / Clear of Clouds
≤ 1,200' AGL (NIGHT) 1 SM / Clear of Clouds* *In Airport Traffic Pattern (otherwise 3-152)
>1.2k' AGL / <10k' MSL (DAY) 1 SM / 3-152
>1.2k' AGL / <10k' MSL (NIGHT) 3 SM / 3-152
Class G Deep Dive:
  • The Helicopter Speed Clause: Under Part 91.155(b)(1), you can legally fly day VFR below 1,200' AGL with less than 1/2 SM visibility, PROVIDED you operate at a speed slow enough to see and avoid all obstructions and traffic.
  • Night Exemption: To use the relaxed "1 SM and Clear of Clouds" rule at night, you must stay within 1/2 mile of the runway or helipad in the airport traffic pattern. Outside that pattern, it immediately defaults to standard night rules (3-152).

About This Project

The Story Behind This Project

During a heavy week of commercial helicopter ground school, I found myself completely overwhelmed trying to track the endless cloud clearances, equipment rules, and unique Class G helicopter exemptions. The data was blurring together, and the standard, dense text walls in the regulations weren't sticking.

In a sudden brainwave, I realized the best way to force myself to truly master the material was to build the exact tool I wished I had.

This interactive visualizer is the result of that deep-dive study session. It strips away the clutter to map standard FAA airspace rules directly to the low-and-slow reality of helicopter operations. It was born out of a personal need to build bulletproof judgment for my own ratings, and it's shared here as an interactive, precise reference for fellow helicopter pilots. Built by a pilot, for pilots. Also note this visualizer will change periodically, as I plan to build it out to include more information or improve the delivery.
